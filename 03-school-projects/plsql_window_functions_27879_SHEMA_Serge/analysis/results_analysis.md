## 2. Diagnostic Analysis: Why Did It Happen?

### Revenue Growth Drivers
1. **Seasonal Patterns**: April showed 47% growth over March, likely due to:
   - Spring promotions
   - Increased marketing spend
   - New customer acquisitions in Asian markets

2. **Regional Performance Variations**:
   - **North America** leads due to:
     - Higher proportion of Premium customers (40% vs 25% in other regions)
     - Earlier market entry and brand recognition
     - Higher average order value ($512 vs $435 other regions)
   
   - **Asia** shows growth potential with:
     - Recent customer acquisitions (4 new customers in Q2)
     - Increasing average spend per customer

3. **Customer Segmentation Insights**:
   - **80/20 Rule Applies**: Top 25% of customers generate 60% of revenue
   - **Premium vs Standard Tiers**: Premium customers spend 3.2x more than Standard
   - **Customer Lifecycle**: Customers acquired in Q1 have higher lifetime value

4. **Product Performance Factors**:
   - **Price Sensitivity**: Mid-range products ($200-$500) performed best
   - **Category Preferences**: Electronics outperformed other categories 3:1
   - **Stock Availability**: All top-performing products maintained healthy inventory levels

### Challenges Identified
1. **Customer Acquisition Cost**: 22% of customers (4/18) made no purchases
2. **Product Underperformance**: 25% of products (5/20) had zero sales
3. **Regional Imbalance**: North America over-indexed compared to other regions
4. **Seasonal Dependence**: Q1 showed slower growth than Q2
